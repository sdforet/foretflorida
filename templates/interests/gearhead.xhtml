Current Ride

2012 Genesis 5.0

After buying my wife a Genesis 3.8 sedan I fell in love with this platform. I was looking for a car which was less than 5 years old, cost less than 30K, and has more than 400 horses. The Genesis 5.0 sedan was the only car that met all of these criteria. I have not been disapointed with my purchase. Since buying the Genesis, I have upgraded the exhaust system by deleting the 2nd cats, upgrading the intermedate pipes to 2.5" true-dual (no-crossover), and tuned the exhaust sound by using 28" Thrush glass packs and Modified DynoMax VT's at the rear. I am also, using K&N highflow filters. I estimate about 20 or so extra HP out of this arrangment since my 0-60 times improved from 5.1 to 4.6 seconds. That makes my family sedan a 450HP sleeper that can outperform any Mustang GT, Camaro SS, Corvette LS1/2, BMW 550, and Mercedes E350/SL550.

Future Ride

2016 Mercedes AMG gt2

Its one thing to have a car that can go fast... its another thing to do it in extreme luxury, while preserving the exileration of a true "driver's" car. Motor Trend named this car it's 2016 Drivers Car of the Year, and I agree it is spectacular. Apart from the high performance and striking goo looks, MT described this car as the greatest true grand tourer. A car that you can not only blast away competition at the track (or red light), but drive comfortably across the country. As an audiophile, the soud of a well tuned powerhouse is almost as imporant as the power is produces, listen to AMG explain the exhaust tuning... "the AMG Dynamic Performance Exhaust can actively bypass its rear muffler, unleashing the... unmistakable symphony of snarling revs, open-throttle cresendos, and crackling deceleration". I can't wait to get my hands on one of these.
